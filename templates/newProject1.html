A Sci-Fi Rock Opera Short Film Written and Directed By Two Fictional Brothers

by Dakkan Abbe

  • Home
  • Updates 1
  • Backers 7
  • Comments 0
  • New York, NY
  • Short Film
  • Embed

A sci-fi rock opera - "Waves" - set on another planet where the inhabitants communicate through wordless music. Inspired by the "The Fantastic Planet"

  • Launched: Nov 9, 2009
  • Funding ended: Feb 7, 2010
  • Remind me

Set on a planet that is inspired by the classic French animated film "The Fantastic Planet," the short film "Waves" will be a beautiful and haunting sci-fi rock opera without words.

The film is about a young "man" on a distant planet who has been falsely accused of murder and is now in a courtroom, facing a jury of his peers. His lover, a beautiful "woman," watches the proceedings from the gallery as witnesses testify, the judge officiates, and the jury deliberates. Flashbacks of the two of them in scenes of blissful communion (more musical than sexual on this planet) will be intercut with the courtroom scenes. Instead of communicating with words, all the "people" on this planet use only pure tones, so that when they open their mouths, beautiful music emanates.

Although the film will be visually surreal, the production will use primarily real sets, costumes and in-camera effects, as opposed to CGI or other digital effects.

The film will be written and directed by Dakkan Abbe (fiftyrubies.com) and the music will be written and performed by Jedediah Smith of My Teenage Stride (myspace.com/myteenagestride)

The estimated running time is five minutes.

The twist of this short is that, in addition to existing in the "real" world (it will be shown online and entered in festivals) it also exists as the back story of a TV show I am creating. This show is about a hot-shot young director named Simon Kunick who made this short film "Waves" at film school, showed it at Sundance, and then had a big-budget feature version of the film produced - which bombed at the box office, trashing his career before it even started. Simon now has to direct reality TV to make a living.
Simon's brother Asher (a rising rock star) composed the music for the short and the feature and suffers a similar fall from grace that forces him to work on reality TV shows as well. My TV series, called "Reality" will follow the brothers as they work on a variety of bizarre reality shows and try to rebuild their once promising careers.

This short "Waves" will actually be credited to the fictional director Simon Kunick and his fictional brother/composer Asher Kunick and the saga of the rise and fall of the brothers is part of the story of the pilot episode of the series.

See? It's a fictional short made by fictional characters set inside a fictional TV show.

Needless to say, it will not be cheap to produce a visually arresting and sophisticated sci-fi short. Even to make it at this price will require an enormous amount of favors and freebies called in, and every penny will go to the set designs, costumes, cameras, lights and essential skilled crew.

Your participation will not only ensure the short will get made, it will also help the TV show it is set inside of get picked up and produced (think prestigious cable network!)

In addition to creating an inspired, beautiful short film, this whole project is an exercise in next-generation pitching - much like Kickstarter.com itself. By actually making the short that exists inside this fictional show, I'm hoping to build a following for the series before the real TV show is pitched. This crazy idea just might work!

You can see some of the things I've written, directed, edited or shot at fiftyrubies.com. In particular you might want to watch the music video for the song Back To Me - it's made entirely of footage from "The Fantastic Planet."

Thanks for your time, please look at the incentives and please please please donate! And don't forget, you are not charged for your pledge unless the entire budget is raised!

Thanks so much!!

You can make it happen!!
